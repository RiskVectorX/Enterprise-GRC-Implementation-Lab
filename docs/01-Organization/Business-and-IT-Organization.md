# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the organizational structure of FinSure Technologies Pvt. Ltd. from both a business and information technology perspective. It identifies key departments, reporting relationships, ownership responsibilities, and governance functions that support the organization's Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) program.

This document establishes accountability for business operations, information security, technology management, and risk ownership.

---

# 2. Organizational Overview

FinSure Technologies operates using a cross-functional organizational model that aligns business operations, technology delivery, cybersecurity, legal compliance, and customer services.

The organizational structure is designed to support secure software development, cloud-native operations, regulatory compliance, and continuous business growth.

# 6. GRC Responsibilities

The Governance, Risk & Compliance program is supported through clearly defined responsibilities.

| Function | Responsibilities |
|----------|------------------|
| Executive Management | Approves security strategy and risk appetite |
| Information Security | Maintains ISMS, policies, risk management activities |
| Legal & Compliance | Ensures regulatory compliance |
| Engineering | Implements secure development practices |
| Cloud Operations | Maintains secure infrastructure |
| Department Managers | Own operational risks within their business units |
| Employees | Comply with organizational policies and security requirements |

# 7. Risk Ownership

Risk ownership is distributed according to operational responsibility.

| Risk Area | Primary Owner |
|-----------|---------------|
| Cloud Infrastructure | Cloud Operations Manager |
| Identity & Access Management | IAM Lead |
| Application Security | Engineering Manager |
| Third-Party Risk | GRC Manager |
| Privacy Risk | Legal & Compliance Manager |
| Incident Management | CISO |
| Business Continuity | Operations Manager |

---

# 8. Asset Ownership

Information assets are assigned accountable owners responsible for their protection and lifecycle management.

Asset owners are responsible for:

- Data classification
- Access approval
- Periodic review
- Risk acceptance
- Regulatory compliance

Detailed ownership is maintained within the Asset Inventory.

---

# 9. Governance Relationships

The GRC program operates through collaboration between multiple departments.

- Executive Management provides strategic oversight.
- Information Security manages the ISMS.
- Engineering implements security requirements.
- Cloud Operations maintains secure infrastructure.
- Legal & Compliance interprets regulatory obligations.
- Internal Audit independently evaluates control effectiveness.
- Business Units identify and manage operational risks.

---

# 10. Segregation of Duties

To reduce operational and security risks, critical responsibilities are separated where practical.

Examples include:

- Developers cannot directly deploy production infrastructure.
- Risk owners are independent from internal auditors.
- Access approvals require managerial authorization.
- Security monitoring is independent of software development.
- Financial approvals require dual authorization.
