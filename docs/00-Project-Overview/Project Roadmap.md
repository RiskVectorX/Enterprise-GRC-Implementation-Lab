# Executive Summary

This roadmap defines the implementation approach for the Enterprise GRC Implementation Lab. It provides a structured view of the project lifecycle, major milestones, and expected deliverables while aligning activities with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) and ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

The roadmap serves as the primary planning document for the engagement and establishes the sequence in which governance, risk management, compliance, and third-party risk management activities will be completed.

---

# Project Vision

Establish a mature Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) program that enables FinSure Technologies Pvt. Ltd. to manage cyber risk effectively, strengthen security governance, and achieve alignment with internationally recognized security standards.

---

# Implementation Approach

The engagement follows a phased implementation strategy.

Each phase builds upon the previous phase to ensure that governance decisions are informed by business context, risk assessments, and regulatory obligations.

# Project Phases

## Phase 0 — Project Foundation

### Objective

Establish project governance, define the engagement, and prepare foundational documentation.

### Deliverables

- Engagement Charter
- Project Roadmap
- Methodology
- Assumptions Register
- Standards & Framework Mapping

---

## Phase 1 — Prepare

### Objective

Understand the organization, define the ISMS scope, establish governance structures, and identify business objectives.

### Deliverables

- Organization Profile
- Business Context
- Governance Structure
- ISMS Scope
- Risk Appetite Statement
- Roles & Responsibilities

---

## Phase 2 — Categorize

### Objective

Identify and classify organizational assets, business processes, and information systems.

### Deliverables

- Asset Inventory
- Asset Classification Matrix
- Business Process Inventory
- Data Flow Diagram
- Information Classification Scheme

---

## Phase 3 — Select

### Objective

Identify risks and select appropriate security controls.

### Deliverables

- Risk Assessment Methodology
- Enterprise Risk Register
- Risk Matrix
- Risk Treatment Plan
- Statement of Applicability
- Control Selection Report

---

## Phase 4 — Implement

### Objective

Implement governance documentation and security controls.

### Deliverables

- Information Security Policy
- Access Control Policy
- Password Policy
- Incident Response Plan
- Vendor Management Policy
- Privacy Controls

---

## Phase 5 — Third-Party Risk Management

### Objective

Develop and implement a structured third-party risk management program.

### Deliverables

- Vendor Inventory
- Vendor Tiering Methodology
- Vendor Risk Assessment
- Security Questionnaire
- Residual Vendor Risk Register
- Continuous Vendor Monitoring Plan

---

## Phase 6 — Assess

### Objective

Evaluate the effectiveness of implemented controls and verify compliance.

### Deliverables

- Internal Audit Checklist
- Audit Report
- Compliance Assessment
- Corrective Action Plan

---

## Phase 7 — Authorize

### Objective

Provide executive management with sufficient information to make informed risk acceptance decisions.

### Deliverables

- Executive Risk Report
- Residual Risk Summary
- Management Review
- Authorization Recommendation

---

## Phase 8 — Monitor

### Objective

Continuously monitor risks, controls, and compliance posture.

### Deliverables

- Continuous Monitoring Strategy
- KPI Dashboard
- KRI Dashboard
- Business Continuity Plan
- Disaster Recovery Plan
- Lessons Learned Report

# Major Milestones

| Milestone | Expected Outcome |
|------------|------------------|
| Project Initiation | Engagement formally established |
| Governance Established | ISMS governance defined |
| Asset Categorization Complete | Business assets identified |
| Risk Assessment Complete | Enterprise risks documented |
| Controls Selected | Security baseline established |
| Policies Implemented | Governance documentation completed |
| Vendor Reviews Complete | Third-party risks evaluated |
| Internal Audit Complete | Control effectiveness verified |
| Executive Authorization | Management approval obtained |
| Continuous Monitoring Operational | Long-term governance established |

---

# Success Indicators

The engagement will be considered successful when:

- All planned deliverables are completed.
- Enterprise risks have documented treatment plans.
- Governance documentation has been approved.
- Security controls are aligned with organizational risks.
- Vendor risk management processes are operational.
- Continuous monitoring processes have been established.

---

# Dependencies

The project phases are sequential.

Each phase depends on the successful completion of the previous phase to ensure governance decisions remain risk-based and aligned with business objectives.
