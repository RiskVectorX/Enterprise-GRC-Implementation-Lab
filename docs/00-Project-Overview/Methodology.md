# Executive Summary

This document defines the methodology used throughout the Enterprise GRC Implementation Lab to identify, assess, treat, and monitor information security risks.

The methodology establishes a structured and repeatable approach that aligns governance activities with business objectives while ensuring consistency across all project deliverables.

The engagement adopts internationally recognized frameworks and standards to support effective decision-making, regulatory compliance, and continual improvement.

---

# Purpose

The methodology provides a standardized approach for:

- Governance implementation
- Risk management
- Security control selection
- Compliance assessment
- Third-party risk management
- Continuous monitoring

All subsequent project deliverables follow the principles established in this document.

---

# Guiding Principles

The engagement is based on the following principles:

## Business Alignment

Security decisions support business objectives rather than hinder business operations.

---

## Risk-Based Decision Making

Security investments are prioritized according to organizational risk exposure.

---

## Defense in Depth

Multiple preventive, detective, and corrective controls are implemented to reduce single points of failure.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Governance and security processes are periodically reviewed and improved based on changing risks, business requirements, and regulatory obligations.

---

## Compliance by Design

Regulatory and contractual requirements are incorporated throughout the implementation lifecycle rather than addressed as a separate activity.

---

# Risk Management Process

The risk management process consists of the following activities:

### 1. Context Establishment

Understand:

- Business objectives
- Regulatory requirements
- Technology environment
- Critical business services
- Stakeholders

---

### 2. Asset Identification

Identify:

- Information assets
- Applications
- Infrastructure
- Cloud services
- Third-party providers
- Business processes

---

### 3. Threat Identification

Evaluate potential threats including:

- External attackers
- Insider threats
- Malware
- Human error
- Supply chain risks
- Natural disasters

---

### 4. Vulnerability Assessment

Identify weaknesses that could be exploited.

Examples include:

- Weak authentication
- Unpatched systems
- Misconfigurations
- Inadequate logging
- Excessive privileges

---

### 5. Risk Assessment

Each identified risk is evaluated based on:

- Likelihood
- Business Impact

The combination of these factors determines the overall inherent risk level.

---

### 6. Risk Treatment

Each risk is assigned one of the following treatment strategies:

- Mitigate
- Transfer
- Avoid
- Accept

Treatment decisions are documented and approved by management.

---

### 7. Control Implementation

Security controls are selected using:

- ISO/IEC 27001 Annex A
- ISO/IEC 27002
- NIST SP 800-53

Controls are implemented according to business priorities and risk levels.

---

### 8. Validation

Implemented controls are validated through:

- Internal audits
- Documentation reviews
- Control testing
- Management reviews

---

### 9. Continuous Monitoring

Security posture is continuously monitored using:

- KPIs
- KRIs
- Risk register reviews
- Vendor monitoring
- Audit findings
- Management reporting

---

# Risk Assessment Approach

Risk assessments performed during this engagement are based on:

- NIST SP 800-30 Rev.1
- ISO 27005 concepts
- ISO 31000 principles

Risk is evaluated using qualitative analysis supported by defined likelihood and impact criteria.

---

# Control Selection Approach

Security controls are selected according to:

- Business requirements
- Regulatory obligations
- Risk treatment decisions
- Industry best practices
- Cost versus benefit
- Organizational maturity
