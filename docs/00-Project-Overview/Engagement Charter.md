# Executive Summary

This document formally establishes the Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) consulting engagement between the consulting team and **FinSure Technologies Pvt. Ltd.**

The objective of this engagement is to design and implement an enterprise-wide Governance, Risk & Compliance program aligned with internationally recognized cybersecurity standards and industry best practices.

The engagement follows the **NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)** lifecycle while developing an Information Security Management System (ISMS) capable of supporting regulatory compliance, organizational resilience, and effective cyber risk management.

---

# Engagement Objectives

The engagement seeks to achieve the following objectives:

- Establish an enterprise Information Security Management System (ISMS)
- Identify and assess information security risks
- Implement risk-based security controls
- Strengthen organizational governance
- Improve third-party risk management
- Support compliance with applicable regulations
- Enhance executive visibility into cyber risk
- Develop sustainable security governance processes

---

# Business Context

FinSure Technologies Pvt. Ltd. is a fictional cloud-native FinTech Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) organization headquartered in Bengaluru, India.

The company provides digital financial services for small and medium-sized businesses, including:

- Digital Payments
- Corporate Expense Management
- Banking API Integrations
- Invoice Automation
- Financial Analytics

Due to the sensitive nature of financial and personal information processed by the organization, effective governance, risk management, and regulatory compliance are considered strategic business priorities.

---

# Scope of Engagement

The engagement includes:

- Information Security Governance
- ISMS Development
- Enterprise Risk Assessment
- Security Policy Development
- Asset Identification & Classification
- Risk Register Development
- Control Selection
- Statement of Applicability (SoA)
- Third-Party Risk Management
- Vendor Risk Assessments
- Internal Security Audits
- Executive Reporting
- Continuous Monitoring Strategy

---

# Out of Scope

The following activities are excluded from this engagement:

- Penetration Testing
- Vulnerability Scanning
- Secure Software Development
- Security Operations Center (SOC) Operations
- Incident Handling Activities
- Digital Forensics
- Red Team Exercises
- Physical Security Assessments

---

# Frameworks & Standards

The engagement aligns with the following standards and frameworks:

- NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)
- NIST SP 800-30 Rev.1
- NIST SP 800-53
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022
- ISO/IEC 27002:2022
- PCI DSS
- GDPR
- Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, India

# Success Criteria

The engagement will be considered successful when:

- Enterprise risks have been identified and documented.
- Risk treatment plans have been established.
- Required security controls have been selected.
- Governance documentation has been completed.
- ISMS documentation is established.
- Executive reporting processes are operational.
- Continuous monitoring processes are defined.

---

# Assumptions

This engagement assumes:

- Executive management actively supports the implementation.
- Required business information is available.
- Stakeholders participate in workshops and reviews.
- Technology inventory remains reasonably stable throughout the engagement.
- Regulatory requirements remain unchanged during implementation.
